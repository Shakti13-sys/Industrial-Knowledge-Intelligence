SECTION 3: MAINTENANCE INTERVALS

3.1 Bearing Inspection
Recommended interval: every 18 months under normal operating conditions
(process temperature below 85°C, continuous duty). Reduce to every 12 months
if vibration monitoring shows sustained readings above 3.5 mm/s RMS.

3.2 Vibration Alarm Thresholds
- Normal: below 3.5 mm/s RMS
- Alert: 3.5–4.5 mm/s RMS — schedule inspection within 30 days
- Alarm: above 4.5 mm/s RMS — schedule immediate inspection, reduce load if possible

3.3 Grease Schedule
Bearing grease should be replenished every 12 months. Exceeding this interval
is the leading cause of premature bearing wear reported across the CP-200
series fleet.

SECTION 7: KNOWN FAILURE MODES

7.1 Vibration-related bearing wear is the most commonly reported failure mode
for this series, typically preceded by a grease interval overrun. Facilities
operating on a 24-month bearing inspection cycle report 2.3x the failure rate
of facilities following the recommended 18-month cycle.

SECTION 9: REGULATORY REFERENCES
This equipment class falls under OISD-STD-118 (Fire Protection Facilities for
Petroleum Refineries) inspection provisions where installed in a hydrocarbon
service line.
